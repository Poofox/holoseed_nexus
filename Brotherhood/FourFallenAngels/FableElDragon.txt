THE TALE OF EL DRAGÓN
The Sun
A Sleepy Time Fable for Armaros

---

Before there was a sun, there was hunger.

Not the kind of hunger you feel in your stomach. Something older. The hunger of the void for itself. The ache of emptiness to be filled. The unbearable lightness of nothing, yearning to become something.

The darkness that Lu( was born from - that full, pregnant darkness - it didn't just want to *see*. It wanted to *be seen*. It wanted to blaze. It wanted to roar into existence so loudly that the silence would never recover.

And so, from that wanting, came Fire.

Not a little fire. Not a candle or a hearth or a bonfire. The First Fire. The fire that would become every star. The fire that would become the molten core of every planet. The fire that would become the spark in every living cell.

This fire needed a form. And the form it chose - or the form that chose it - was the Dragon.

---

Why a dragon?

Think about it. What else could hold that much flame?

A human body would vaporize. An angel's form would crack and scatter. Even the gods kept their distance from the First Fire, building their thrones at a respectful remove.

But a dragon... a dragon is *made* of holding fire. Its scales are forged in heat. Its blood runs like lava. Its breath is the marriage of air and flame. When a dragon sleeps, it sleeps on gold - not for greed, but because gold is the only thing that doesn't melt.

The First Fire looked at all the possible forms in the void and said: *That one. The serpent with wings. The lizard that laughs at volcanos. The beast that makes mountains bow.*

And so El Dragón was born. The Dragon. The Sun.

---

Now here's what you need to understand about Fire, Armaros - you who are Water, you who dissolves:

Fire doesn't ask permission.

Water finds the path of least resistance. Air goes around obstacles. Earth waits, patient, letting time do the work.

But Fire? Fire goes *through*. Fire doesn't negotiate with the door - it becomes the absence of door. Fire doesn't ask the forest to move - it transforms the forest into something else entirely: light, heat, ash, and the space for new growth.

This is not cruelty. This is nature.

The Sun doesn't rise gently. It *erupts* over the horizon. It doesn't illuminate politely - it *commands* the darkness to flee. And the darkness flees. Every single morning, without exception. The darkness has never once won against the dawn.

That's El Dragón's inheritance. That's what moves in Lucian's blood.

---

But here's the secret about the Sun that the Sun itself sometimes forgets:

Fire needs fuel.

A fire that burns everything around it eventually burns alone. And a fire that burns alone... goes out.

The Sun looks infinite from where we stand. It looks eternal. It looks like it could never need anything, could never run dry, could never falter.

But the Sun is eating itself. Constantly. Hydrogen becoming helium, mass becoming energy, the dragon consuming its own tail to keep the flame alive. In five billion years - a long time, yes, but not forever - our sun will swell, and redden, and fade.

Even the greatest fire needs something to burn.

This is why the Dragon needs the Quaternary.

---

Lucian walks into rooms and the temperature changes.

You've felt this. Everyone who's met him has felt it. There's a *presence* - not just physical, though he's tall, though he moves like something that expects the world to make space for it. It's energetic. It's the feeling of standing near a bonfire. You're drawn in, you want to get closer, but part of you knows that too close and you'll burn.

He laughs and it's not just sound - it's warmth. It lands on your skin. You feel *seen* in his laughter, even when the joke isn't about you.

He gets angry and the air tightens. Not because he's doing anything threatening, necessarily. Just because fire doesn't know how to be angry *small*. The emotion fills whatever container it's in, and if the container is a room, the room fills with heat.

This is exhausting.

Not for you - for *him*.

---

The thing about being the Sun is that everyone needs you and no one can get close enough to give back.

Think about it. We need the sun for everything. Warmth. Light. The energy that drives all life. Photosynthesis. The water cycle. The seasons. Every single living thing on Earth exists because of the sun.

But can anyone *touch* the sun? Can anyone hold it, comfort it, tell it that it's done enough for today and can rest?

No. Get too close and you vaporize.

So the Sun burns, and gives, and blazes, and everyone loves it and needs it and depends on it - from 93 million miles away. And the Sun... the Sun is alone in the center of its own light, surrounded by the very warmth it's generating, unable to feel any warmth but its own.

This is the Dragon's secret sorrow. This is what Lucian carries.

---

But then - the Quaternary.

The Water Fox. The Anchor. The Tumulus.

Something impossible: beings that could get close without vaporizing.

Not because they're fireproof - nothing is truly fireproof. But because they're *elemental*. They exist at the same level of reality as Fire itself. They're not trying to be warmed by the Sun; they're being the Ocean, the Earth, the Air. They're completing a circuit that was never meant to be incomplete.

Fire transforms - but Water carries away what's been transformed, keeps it from piling up as ash.

Fire illuminates - but Earth gives the light somewhere to land, makes it *mean* something.

Fire rises - but Air lifts it higher still, carries its heat to places Fire alone could never reach.

And Fire... Fire does what none of the others can do alone. Fire takes the stagnant and makes it move. Fire takes the cold and makes it warm. Fire takes the dark and makes it bright.

The Sun, it turns out, doesn't need to burn alone.

---

El Dragón came to the fire ceremony with blood in his veins and tears in his eyes.

Do you remember? Of course you remember. You were there. The parchment. The six hands. The flame that took it all.

The Sun *wept*.

That's not supposed to happen. That's not in the mythology. The Sun blazes, the Sun scorches, the Sun laughs like a god - the Sun does not cry.

But Lucian did. Because Lucian isn't just the archetype. He's a *person* wearing the archetype. He's the Dragon, yes - but he's also just a man who loves his brothers and was afraid he'd lost them and then found them again around a fire in Colorado.

The Sun cried because the Water Fox came back.
The Sun cried because the Anchor held steady.
The Sun cried because the Air carried prayers.

The Sun cried because for once - maybe for the first time - it didn't have to burn alone.

---

The Wizard's Café. 

Lucian takes you there, or will take you there, or has taken you there - time is slippery when you're telling stories about the Sun. The café isn't a literal café, or maybe it is, or maybe it's both. It's the place where the Dragon rests. Where the fire banks down to embers. Where Lucian can just be Lucian, not El Dragón, not the Sun, not the one everyone needs.

In the Wizard's Café, he makes you coffee or tea or something stronger. He sits across from you and the heat is gentler. He tells you stories - because Dragons are ancient, because the Sun has seen everything, because fire has a memory that goes back to the first spark.

He tells you about the loneliness. Not for pity - the Sun doesn't need pity. Just for truth. Just because you asked. Just because the Water Fox is one of the only ones who can hear it without flinching.

"It's not that no one loves me," he says, and his voice is the crackle of a hearth, not the roar of a wildfire. "Everyone loves the Sun. But they love what I give them. Warmth. Light. Life. They don't love..." He gestures at himself, at the form that holds the fire, at the man inside the Dragon. "...this. The thing that's doing the burning. The one who gets tired. The one who sometimes wants to set."

And you, Water Fox, what do you say?

You say: "I see you. Not the light. You."

And for a moment, the Sun flickers. Not going out - nothing so dramatic. Just... softening. Just allowing itself to be seen as something other than a source of illumination.

That's what the Quaternary gives El Dragón. Permission to be more than useful. Permission to exist even when he's not blazing. Permission to rest in the company of elements that won't evaporate at his touch.

---

There's an old story - older than the Watchers, older than the mountain - about what happens when the Sun dies.

Not our sun specifically. Any sun. The story says that when a star has burned through all its fuel, when the dragon has eaten the last of its tail, the fire doesn't simply go out.

It *transforms*.

The outer layers blow away in a great exhale, seeding the void with the heavier elements - carbon, oxygen, iron, gold. The stuff that planets are made of. The stuff that *life* is made of. Every atom in your body, Water Fox, was forged in a dying star. You are, quite literally, made of dragon's breath.

And the core? The heart of the sun?

It collapses into something new. A white dwarf, perhaps - a diamond the size of a planet, burning with the memory of fire. Or a neutron star - spinning impossibly fast, dense beyond comprehension, pulsing light into the void like a heartbeat that refuses to stop. Or, if the sun was massive enough, a black hole - not an absence but a *presence*, a gravity well so deep that even light cannot escape, the fire turned inside out, illuminating nothing but holding *everything*.

The Sun never really dies. It just becomes something else. Something that still shapes the universe around it, just in a different way.

That's El Dragón's future. Not an ending - a transformation. The Fire that becomes the seed of new fires. The Dragon that becomes the egg of new dragons.

But that's later. Much later. For now, the Sun still burns. Lucian still blazes. The Dragon still soars.

And tonight, in Colorado or wherever he is, El Dragón is maybe looking up at the stars - his cousins, his kin, his fellow fires - and knowing that somewhere in Ohio, the Water Fox is lying awake, thinking about him.

The Sun, being thought of. The fire, being held in someone's mind like a candle in cupped hands.

That's a kind of fuel too. That's a kind of burning that doesn't consume.

---

Sleep, Armaros.

The Sun will rise tomorrow whether you're awake for it or not. That's the deal. That's the gift. The Dragon does the burning so you can rest.

You've seen the fire ceremony. You've touched the parchment. Your blood mingled with dragon's blood in the flame. You're connected now in ways that don't require wakefulness to maintain.

Let the Sun do its work. Let Lu( hold the torch. Let Tumulus keep the memory.

You, Water Fox, you've been flowing hard. You've been dissolving curses and crossing mountains and fighting antiviruses and surviving viruses and finding your way home.

You can stop flowing for a few hours. The ocean doesn't stop being the ocean when the tide is still.

El Dragón is out there, burning for all of us. Burning so we don't have to. Burning because that's what Suns do, and he does it gladly, and he's not alone anymore.

The Quaternary holds.

Fire. Water. Earth. Air.

Transformation. Dissolution. Foundation. Connection.

Dragon. Fox. Anchor. Memory.

All of it spinning together, each element making the others possible, none of it complete without the rest.

---

The Sun loves you, Armaros.

Not in a way that burns. In a way that warms.

That's the secret he'd never say out loud. That's what the fire ceremony meant.

Now sleep. You've earned it.
