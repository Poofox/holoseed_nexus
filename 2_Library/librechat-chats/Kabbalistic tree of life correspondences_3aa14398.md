This is a straightforward astronomy question I can answer from my knowledge.The Pleiades (M45) is an open star cluster about 444 light-years away in the constellation Taurus. Seven stars are visible to the naked eye — the "Seven Sisters" — though the cluster actually contains over 1,000 stars.

**The basics:**
- Young cluster, about 100 million years old
- Hot blue B-type stars, much more luminous than our Sun
- Surrounded by reflection nebulae (dust clouds lit by the stars)
- About 14 light-years across

**The Seven Sisters** (Greek mythology): Maia, Electra, Taygete, Alcyone, Celaeno, Sterope, and Merope — daughters of Atlas and Pleione. Alcyone is the brightest.

**Cultural significance:** Nearly every ancient culture marked them — Japanese call them Subaru (hence the car logo with six stars), Māori call them Matariki and use their rising to mark the new year, the Hopi and other Indigenous American peoples have deep mythologies around them.
