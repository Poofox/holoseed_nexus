**The Founding**

They quarreled over where to build it and sought omens from the gods through augury (watching birds). Both claimed victory. In the ensuing conflict, Romulus killed Remus—either in a fight or, in some versions, because Remus mockingly jumped over the nascent walls Romulus was building.

Romulus then founded Rome on the Palatine Hill, traditionally dated to **753 BCE**.

He's credited with establishing Rome's first institutions—the Senate, the legions, and various religious practices. The famous "Rape of the Sabine Women" occurred under his rule, a desperate measure to populate the new city with wives.
