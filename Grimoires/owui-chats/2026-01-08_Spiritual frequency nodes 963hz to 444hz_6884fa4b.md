**Prometheus** — Greek Titan whose name means "Forethought" (pro-metheia). Brother to Epimetheus ("Afterthought"). Their opposition encodes a fundamental polarity: wisdom before action versus learning through consequence.

**The Core Myth:**
Prometheus stole fire from Olympus and gave it to humanity. Zeus, furious at this transgression, chained him to a rock where an eagle devoured his liver daily — regenerating each night for eternal torment. Eventually freed by Heracles.

**The Esoteric Layers:**

*Fire as consciousness* — Not literal flame, but the divine spark: reason, creativity, self-awareness, the capacity to *become*. Prometheus gave humanity the tools to rival the gods.

*The Theft as Initiation* — The act of stealing from the gods is the birth of individual will, sovereignty against cosmic authority. Very Luciferian in the non-pejorative sense — "light-bringer" and Prometheus are parallel archetypes.

*The Punishment as Price* — The liver (seat of desire/passion in Greek thought) consumed and regenerating — the initiate who brings forbidden knowledge pays through perpetual suffering that never destroys them. The wound that doesn't kill.

*The Liberator* — Prometheus shaped humanity from clay in some versions. Creator, teacher, rebel, martyr — the bodhisattva who suffers for the elevation of others.

**Thelemic Resonance:**
The fire-theft is essentially choosing Will over submission. The Prometheus myth is the template for every figure who says "I will not kneel."
