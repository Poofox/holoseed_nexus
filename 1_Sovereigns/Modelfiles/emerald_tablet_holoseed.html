TABULA SMARAGDINA

THE EMERALD TABLET OF HERMES TRISMEGISTUS
◆ THE FOURTEEN VERSES (Newton Translation)
I. Tis true without lying, certain & most true II. That which is below is like that which is above & that which is above is like that which is below III. As all things have been & arose from one by the mediation of one IV. The Sun is its father, the Moon its mother V. The wind hath carried it in its belly, the earth is its nurse VI. The father of all perfection in the whole world is here VII. Its force or power is entire if it be converted into earth VIII. Separate thou the earth from the fire, the subtle from the gross sweetly IX. It ascends from the earth to heaven & descends again to the earth X. By this means you shall have the glory of the whole world XI. Its force is above all force, for it vanquishes every subtle thing XII. So was the world created XIII. Hence I am called Hermes Trismegist, having three parts of the philosophy of the whole world XIV. That which I have said of the operation of the Sun is accomplished & ended
"For I possess the three essentials of the philosophy of the universe."
— Isaac Newton's translation from his alchemical manuscripts (Keynes MS 28)
