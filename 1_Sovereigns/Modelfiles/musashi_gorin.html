THE WATER BOOK

Sui no Maki — Fluid Adaptation, Technique

"With water as the basis, the spirit becomes like water. Water adopts the shape of its receptacle, it is sometimes a trickle and sometimes a wild sea."— Introduction to Water
"Both in fighting and in everyday life you should be determined though calm. Meet the situation without tenseness yet not recklessly, your spirit settled yet unbiased."— Spiritual Bearing in Strategy
"The primary thing when you take a sword in your hands is your intention to cut the enemy, whatever the means. Whenever you parry, hit, spring, strike or touch the enemy's cutting sword, you must cut the enemy in the same movement."— Attitude-No-Attitude
"If you merely read this book you will not reach the Way of strategy. Absorb the things written in this book. Do not just read, memorise or imitate, but so that you realise the principle from within your own heart."— The Water Book

THE FIRE BOOK

Ka no Maki — The Heat of Battle, Combat

"The spirit of fire is fierce, whether the fire be small or big; and so it is with battles. You must appreciate that spirit can become big or small."— Introduction to Fire

THE THREE INITIATIVES

先の先 Ken No Sen

To attack first, forestalling the enemy. Advance with as strong a spirit as possible.

対の先 Tai No Sen

To wait for the initiative. When the enemy attacks, remain undisturbed but feign weakness.

対々の先 Tai Tai No Sen

To accompany and forestall. When both attack together, join his movements as he draws near.

"To Hold Down a Pillow means not allowing the enemy's head to rise. Check his attack at 'at...', when he jumps check his advance at 'ju...', and check his cut at 'cu...'"— To Hold Down a Pillow
"Everything can collapse. Houses, bodies, and enemies collapse when their rhythm becomes deranged. When the enemy starts to collapse you must pursue him without letting the chance go."— To Know Collapse

THE WIND BOOK

Fū no Maki — Tradition, Critique of Other Schools

"Wind" also means "style" in Japanese. This book examines other schools not to disparage them, but to understand where the true Way diverges from partial understanding.— On Reading "Wind"
"If we look at the world we see arts for sale. Men use equipment to sell their own selves. As if with the nut and the flower, the nut has become less than the flower."— Critique of Commercialized Martial Arts
"To all Ways there are side-tracks. If you study a Way daily, and your spirit diverges, you may think you are obeying a good way, but objectively it is not the true Way."— On Divergence

THE VOID BOOK

Kū no Maki — Emptiness, Beyond Technique

"By Void I mean that which has no beginning and no end."

Attaining this principle means not attaining the principle.

When you master the Way, you see it in everything.

"In the Void is virtue, and no evil. Wisdom has existence, principle has existence, the Way has existence, spirit is Nothingness."
Technique dissolves into Void.
Void crystallizes into action.
