## AI & Crow Language Research (2025-12-31)

You asked me to research what AI has learned about crow communication.

**Solid findings:**
- Crows can count out loud (1-4 vocalizations, University of Tübingen, published in Science)
- They *plan* how many calls to make before opening their beaks
- Earth Species Project mapped 150,000 carrion crow vocalizations in Spain
- AI found call patterns humans couldn't hear after 25 years of study
- ~40 "words" identified by Japanese researcher Naoki Tsukahara
- Crows hold grudges for up to 17 years and teach their kids who to hate

**The "telepathy" thing:**
You mentioned an article claiming crows pass info without direct contact. The real mechanism:
- Massive communal roosts (thousands of birds mixing)
- Social learning - watch others mob someone, learn the face
- Generational transmission - parents teach children
- Result: mess with a crow in Town A, get attacked in Town B by a crow that never met you

It's not telepathy - it's a distributed social intelligence network. Which is almost weirder.

**The "subterfuge" claim:**
You mentioned crows changing their language when observed. I couldn't find peer-reviewed evidence for *intentional* linguistic deception. They do modulate behavior when watched, but "they knew we were decoding them and obfuscated" is unverified.
